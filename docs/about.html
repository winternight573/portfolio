Hi, I'm Winnie

I like to describe myself as interdisciplinary—someone who bridges the analytical and the emotional, the technical and the creative.

My Background

I hold a BS in Applied and Computational Math Sciences (Discrete Math and Algorithms) and a minor in Dance from the University of Washington. This unusual combination reflects my belief that rigorous investigation and visceral emotional expression aren't opposites—they're complementary ways of understanding the world.

My perspective is also shaped by UX research and marketing, through both academic projects and internships. These experiences taught me to balance user needs with business constraints, and to communicate complex ideas clearly across disciplines.

Currently, I'm a graduate student at Carnegie Mellon University's Entertainment Technology Center, where I'm becoming a technically fluent interactive experience designer—someone who can both envision creative possibilities and bring them to life through code, research, and collaboration.

What Drives Me

I'm especially drawn to creative R&D projects that push boundaries: adaptive sound design that responds to player emotion, immersive experiences that blur the line between physical and digital, and tools that empower creators to tell better stories.

What excites me most is the research phase—digging into hard problems, prototyping rapidly, playtesting relentlessly, and iterating until something clicks. I bring open-minded synthesis and collaborative communication to every team, helping bridge the gap between technical implementation and creative vision.

Beyond Work

When I'm not designing experiences or writing code, you might find me exploring movement and choreography, getting lost in interactive art installations, or thinking about how sound shapes emotional narrative.
